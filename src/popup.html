Specific instructions for Microsoft accounts

  1. Go to mysignins.microsoft.com/security-info and click Add sign-in method (if you don't see it, click 'Security info' in the left sidebar).
  2. Select Microsoft Authenticator → Set up a different authentication app → Next → Can't scan the QR code? → copy the secret key.
  3. Open ReDD 2FA in a new tab, add an account and paste the secret key. Then copy one of the codes that ReDD 2FA generates.
  4. Go back to the tab with your Microsoft account, click Next, and paste the code from ReDD 2FA. Done! 🥳

Note: When you next log in, your Microsoft account will probably still default to the Authenticator app. Click "I can't use my Microsoft Authenticator app right now" → Use a verification code.

General process

1

Get a secret key from your service

From the service you want to use with ReDD 2FA (e.g. your Microsoft account), you need a 'secret key'. They're often shown as a QR code, where you need to click 'can't scan?'.

2

Add the secret key to ReDD 2FA

In ReDD 2FA, click 'Add account', give it a name you'll remember (e.g. 'My University 2FA'), and paste the secret key you got from your service. ReDD 2FA will start generating rotating one-time passwords.

3

Give a code from ReDD 2FA to your service

Copy a generated code back into the service you want to use with ReDD 2FA. This confirms the linking of the two services. Well done! 🥳

2-factor authentication (2FA) adds a security layer beyond your password. This makes digital life much more secure, because a hacker cannot get into your accounts with your password alone.

ReDD 2FA uses the most common approach to 2FA, namely time-based one-time passwords (TOTP). You get a secret key from e.g. your Microsoft account, pass it to ReDD 2FA, and it will generate a unique 6-digit code for you every 30 seconds. You can then copy this code and paste it into your (e.g.) Microsoft account to log in.

ReDD 2FA is safe, secure, and open-source. It runs completely locally on your device, and encrypts your secret keys with your master passphrase / touch ID, which never leaves your device. It is developed by computer scientists at the University of Oxford (Dr Ulrik Lyngs) and University of Maastricht (Dr Konrad Kollnig, Henry Tari), as part of their work with the Reduce Digital Distraction Project. The code is available at https://github.com/ulyngs/redd-2fa.

You have two options:

1. Tap the ⋮ button for any of your accounts in ReDD 2FA, choose Edit, and copy the secret key into your new app.

2. Use Export backup above and choose Plain text URIs to create a file you can import into most authenticator apps.

Export Backup

Choose a password to protect the backup file.

⚠️ This will export all secrets as unencrypted plain text. Anyone with this file can access your accounts. Only use this for migrating to another authenticator app.

Import Backup

Restore accounts from a backup file. Supports backups from ReDD 2FA, as well as plain otpauth:// URI text file exports from other authenticator apps.

Enable Touch ID?

Unlock ReDD 2FA with your fingerprint instead of typing your passphrase each time.

When prompted, choose Google Password Manager (or another password manager like 1Password).

Do not select "Windows Hello", as this currently does not support secure processing of your fingerprint from a browser extension.
